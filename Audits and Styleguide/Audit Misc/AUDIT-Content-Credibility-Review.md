# SETD5 Syndrome Site Content Credibility Audit

**Date:** March 8, 2026
**Scope:** All content pages on setd5syndrome.live
**Auditor context:** This audit reviews claims made on a site run by a parent (not a credentialed professional) of a child with SETD5 Syndrome.

---

## Summary

The site is **exceptionally well-hedged overall**. The qualifying-for-services, medical-emergency-summary, and about pages are model examples of how a parent-run resource should present information. Most pages use language like "may qualify," "some individuals," "has been reported," and "discuss with your care team" throughout.

The findings below represent the relatively small number of items that could benefit from additional softening, sourcing, or revision across the entire site. Most are minor.

**Pages with zero or near-zero flags:** qualifying-for-services.html, medical-emergency-summary.html, about.html, setd5-research-2020-to-present.html, sources-references.html, helpful-links.html, family-stories.html, share-your-story.html, suggest-a-resource.html, index.html, family-toolkit.html, printable-handouts.html

## Flagged Items by Page

---

### understanding-setd5-syndrome.html

**1. NEEDS SOURCE**
> "Published studies have documented approximately 75 or more individuals with SETD5 Syndrome as of 2025."

Why flagged: Specific numerical claim without citation. The number may also be outdated.
Suggested revision: "As of [year], published studies have documented approximately [X] individuals with SETD5 Syndrome (source: [cite specific review or tally])." Or link to the sources-references page.

---

**2. NEEDS SOURCE**
> "The SETD5 Syndrome Facebook support group has grown to over 1,100 members worldwide."

Why flagged: Specific membership count that changes over time; no citation.
Suggested revision: Add "as of [month/year]" and consider noting the source (e.g., the group page or the TMC dissertation that surveyed 51 members across 12 countries).

---

**3. NEEDS SOURCE**
> "Most reported cases of SETD5 Syndrome are de novo."

Why flagged: "Most" is a quantifier that implies a specific proportion. This is likely accurate but would benefit from a citation.
Suggested revision: "In published case series, the majority of reported SETD5 variants have been de novo (research suggests [cite]; individual families should confirm inheritance through genetic counseling)."

---

**4. NEEDS SOURCE**
> "A happy disposition and sociable demeanor have been reported in the literature and by families, though this varies."

Why flagged: References "the literature" without citation. The hedging ("though this varies") is good.
Suggested revision: Add a parenthetical citation to the specific study or studies that reported this, or change to: "Some families and published case reports have described a happy disposition and sociable demeanor, though this varies widely."

---

**5. NEEDS SOURCE**
> "Reduced bone density is a concern (first documented in SETD5 Syndrome in 2021)."

Why flagged: Specific year claim ("first documented...in 2021") without citation.
Suggested revision: Add citation to the specific 2021 paper, or say "reported in published research beginning around 2021."

---

**6. VERIFIED (well-hedged)**
> "Moyamoya disease (a rare condition affecting blood flow to the brain) has been reported in a very small number of individuals with SETD5 Syndrome in published literature; this appears to be uncommon and routine screening is not currently established."

This is an example of excellent hedging. No change needed.

### school-iep-guide.html

**7. NEEDS SOFTENING**
> "Children with SETD5 Syndrome typically meet eligibility criteria under IDEA."

Why flagged: "Typically" implies near-certainty about eligibility, which is an educational/legal determination made individually.
Suggested revision: "Given the developmental features associated with SETD5 Syndrome, many children may meet eligibility criteria under IDEA. Eligibility is determined through individual evaluation by the school district."

---

**8. NEEDS SOFTENING**
> "Most children with SETD5 Syndrome will qualify for an IEP."

Why flagged: Same issue. "Most...will qualify" sounds like a guaranteed outcome. This was previously softened from an earlier version but could go further.
Suggested revision: "For children with SETD5 Syndrome, some children may qualify for an IEP based on the results of a comprehensive evaluation. Eligibility is determined through individual evaluation by the school district."

---

**9. VERIFIED**
> "IEP services are legal entitlements established by federal and state law."

This is a factual statement about IDEA. No change needed.

---

**10. VERIFIED**
> "If you disagree with the school's evaluation, you have the right to request an Independent Educational Evaluation at the school district's expense. The school must either fund the IEE or file for a due process hearing to justify their own evaluation."

This accurately describes IDEA procedural safeguards. No change needed.

### transition-to-adulthood.html

**11. NEEDS SOURCE**
> "research has found that young adults can see benefits discontinued at that review"

Why flagged: References "research" without citation. This is a significant claim that families may act on.
Suggested revision: "Research has documented that some young adults see benefits discontinued at the age-18 review, even when the underlying disability has not changed (source: [cite GAO report or SSA data])."

---

**12. NEEDS SOURCE**
> "Six months or more in advance is a timeframe commonly suggested by disability advocates."

Why flagged: Attributes guidance to "disability advocates" without naming them.
Suggested revision: "Disability advocacy organizations such as The Arc and NDRN suggest beginning this process at least six months before your child turns 18."

---

**13. NEEDS SOFTENING / VERIFY CURRENCY**
> "An ABLE account must be opened before the individual turns 26. Once age 26 is reached, an ABLE account cannot be established."

Why flagged: The ABLE Age Adjustment Act (signed December 2024) raised the age-of-onset requirement from 26 to 46, effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2025. This text may now be outdated.
Suggested revision: Verify the current age cutoff and update accordingly. As of 2026, the language should reflect the new age-46 threshold if the law has taken effect. Add "as of [date]; check ablenrc.org for the most current rules."

---

**14. NEEDS SOURCE**
> "adult disability services in most states have waiting lists of years or even decades"

Why flagged: "Years or even decades" is a strong claim. While widely reported, it needs attribution.
Suggested revision: "Adult disability services in many states have significant waiting lists. Reports from The Arc and state disability agencies have documented waits of several years or longer in some states."

---

**15. VERIFIED**
> "Your child still has the right to school services under IDEA until age 21 or high school graduation."

This is a factual statement about IDEA Part B. No change needed.

---

### understanding-genetic-report.html

**24. NEEDS SOFTENING**
> "Clinicians treat this the same as pathogenic." (referring to Likely Pathogenic)

Why flagged: Overgeneralization. While generally true, clinical decisions vary.
Suggested revision: "In clinical practice, likely pathogenic variants are generally treated with the same level of concern as pathogenic variants, though individual clinical decisions may vary."

---

**25. NEEDS SOFTENING**
> "If your child's variant appears frequently here [gnomAD], it's probably harmless. If rare or absent, that supports disease-causing."

Why flagged: Oversimplified interpretation guidance. Allele frequency is one factor among many.
Suggested revision: "If a variant appears frequently in gnomAD, that is one piece of evidence suggesting it may be benign. Rarity in gnomAD can support (but does not confirm) pathogenicity. Variant interpretation should be done by a genetics professional."

---

**26. NEEDS SOFTENING**
> "This is one of the most valuable resources available to SETD5 Syndrome families." (Simons Searchlight genetic counseling)

Why flagged: Superlative evaluative claim.
Suggested revision: "This is a widely used resource for SETD5 Syndrome families." Or: "Many families find this to be a helpful resource."

### setd5-research-2020-to-present.html

**27. VERIFIED**
This page is exemplary. Every statistic is attributed to a specific published study. Each research summary includes appropriate caveats ("case report -- findings apply to one patient," "preprint -- not yet peer-reviewed," "early-stage research"). The "Relevance for families" sections consistently direct families to their care team. No changes recommended.

## Overall Assessment

| Verdict | Count |
|---------|-------|
| VERIFIED | Vast majority of content across all pages |
| NEEDS SOURCE | 7 items (mostly adding citations to already-accurate claims) |
| NEEDS SOFTENING | 8 items (mostly minor wording adjustments) |
| NEEDS SOFTENING / VERIFY CURRENCY | 2 items (ABLE age cutoff -- potentially outdated) |
| REMOVE OR REWRITE | 0 items |

**No content on this site rises to the level of REMOVE OR REWRITE.** The site consistently avoids stating causes, mechanisms, or treatment outcomes as established fact. It does not recommend or evaluate medical interventions. It does not describe what a diagnosis means for a child's future in definitive terms. Legal and benefits information is presented carefully with appropriate hedging.

The 15 flagged items above are refinements, not corrections. This is an unusually well-written parent resource.

Site Protection Audit — March 2026

Full audit across Area 1 (Disclaimer), Area 2 (External Links), and Area 3 (Outdated Content). All pages reviewed directly from source files.

Area 1 — Disclaimer Presence & Strength

Sitewide Footer (all pages) — NEEDS IMPROVEMENT

Current text: "This site is not affiliated with any medical institution or research organization. For educational purposes only."

Issues: The universal footer disclaimer identifies what the site is not (not affiliated), but fails to (1) state that the author is a parent, not a professional; (2) explicitly say the content is not medical, legal, or educational advice; (3) direct visitors to consult professionals. A first-time visitor reading any page would not learn from the footer alone that this is parent-created peer content.

Suggested revision: "This site is run by a parent of a child with SETD5 Syndrome. Content is for informational purposes only and is not medical, legal, or educational advice. Always consult qualified professionals for decisions about your child's care, education, or benefits."

school-iep-guide.html — NEEDS IMPROVEMENT (highest risk)

Issue: This page uses legal-sounding language throughout: FAPE, LRE, Child Find, "legally required," "entitled to," citations to 34 C.F.R. § 300.148. It is the most legally authoritative-sounding page on the site. Yet it has no in-page disclaimer — only the footer and a single "consult" reference deep in the page.

Suggested fix: Add a prominent disclaimer callout at the top of the page, before the first section. Something like: "This guide was written by a parent, not an attorney or special education advocate. It reflects our experience and research, not legal advice. Laws, procedures, and rights vary by state and individual circumstance. For guidance specific to your situation, consider working with your state's Parent Training and Information Center or a special education advocate."

disability-discounts-guide.html — NEEDS IMPROVEMENT

Issue: This page covers SSI, Medicaid, LIHEAP, and other federal benefits — all areas where eligibility rules change frequently and incorrect information could affect real decisions. The "About this guide" intro only addresses documentation requirements. There is no statement that this is not benefits advice, that eligibility varies and changes, or that families should verify with the relevant agency.

Suggested fix: Expand the "About this guide" callout: "This guide was compiled by a parent and is not benefits or legal advice. Program availability, eligibility rules, and discount terms change frequently. Always verify current details directly with the program or agency before relying on anything listed here."

transition-to-adulthood.html — NEEDS IMPROVEMENT

Issue: This page covers guardianship, power of attorney, supported decision-making, ABLE accounts, and SSI — all areas with significant legal and financial complexity. The page uses "consult" directives within sections, which is good, but has no up-front disclaimer identifying the author as a non-professional or noting that these are legally variable processes. A reader skimming the guardianship section could easily absorb it as authoritative guidance.

Suggested fix: Add a disclaimer at the top of the page, before the Healthcare Transition section: "This page was written by a parent. Legal and financial planning for adulthood is complex and varies by state. The information here is meant to help you ask better questions — not to substitute for an attorney, financial advisor, or benefits counselor."

family-toolkit.html — NEEDS IMPROVEMENT

Issue: The Family Toolkit is the hub page linking to all guides — the legal, benefits, education, and medical pages. It has no in-page disclaimer at all (only the footer). A visitor landing here for the first time has no indication that the linked guides are parent-created peer content, not professional resources.

Suggested fix: Add a brief sentence near the top: "All guides on this site are written by a parent of a child with SETD5 Syndrome. Nothing here is medical, legal, or benefits advice — always work with qualified professionals for decisions about your child."

Area 2 — External Link Credibility

biorxiv.org preprints — research.html — REVIEW

Issue: At least one link goes to biorxiv.org, a preprint server where studies have not been peer-reviewed. There is no indication in the link text or description that this is a preprint rather than a published study. Families reading the research page may not know the difference.

Suggested fix: Add a label "(preprint — not yet peer-reviewed)" next to any biorxiv or bioRxiv link. Consider adding a one-sentence explainer at the top of the research page: "Some linked studies are preprints, meaning they have not yet completed peer review."

fullmindlearning.com — school-iep-guide.html — REVIEW

Issue: Two links in the school IEP guide go to a blog post on fullmindlearning.com, which is a private tutoring company. The post ("IEP Examples for Teachers, Admins, and Parents") is not from a government source, disability law organization, or parent advocacy group. Its commercial context means it may prioritize marketing over accuracy and could be updated or removed without notice.

Suggested fix: Replace with a link from Understood.org, Wrightslaw.com, or the PACER Center (pacer.org), which are non-commercial and more authoritative sources for IEP examples and guides.

Retailer links (Walmart+, Sam's Club, Kroger, Amazon) — disability-discounts-guide.html — REVIEW

Issue: Four links go to commercial retailer pages describing SNAP/EBT discounts or discount membership programs. Retailer policies change frequently and without notice, and these pages may move, update, or be discontinued. Linking directly to retailer pages for benefits information is less stable than linking to official USDA SNAP or SSA program pages.

Suggested fix: Consider replacing retailer-specific links with the official USDA SNAP retailer locator (fns.usda.gov) or linking to each retailer's current accessibility/EBT page with a "verify this is current" note. The existing "programs may change" language in the page is good — extend it to flag these retailer links specifically.

University dissertation — research.html — REVIEW

Suggested fix: Label it "(graduate dissertation, UT Health)" so readers know it differs from peer-reviewed journal citations.

Conference abstract — research.html — REVIEW

Issue: One link goes to abstracts.eurospe.org (European Society for Paediatric Endocrinology conference abstracts). A conference abstract is preliminary work — not peer-reviewed, often not yet published, and sometimes not updated if the research evolved.

Audit standard: This site is written by a non-credentialed author for a patient and family audience. The standard is not whether a statement is clinically accurate, but whether it could be misread as clinical guidance, directional advice, or a verified fact about the current state of medical practice or science.

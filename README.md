# SETD5 Syndrome — Research & Resources Website

A collection of plain-language guides for families, caregivers, and researchers navigating a SETD5 Syndrome diagnosis. The site is designed to be accessible to people without a medical background, while remaining accurate and grounded in published research.

The site is live at **[setd5syndrome.live](https://setd5syndrome.live)**.

---

## Pages

### `index.html` — Home Page
The main landing page. Links to all guides and includes a note for international readers about US-specific content.

### `setd5-newly-diagnosed-guide.html` — Newly Diagnosed Guide
A compassionate, practical guide for families who have just received a SETD5 diagnosis. Covers what to expect, where to start, and how to find support.

### `setd5-parent-report-2020-to-present.html` — SETD5 Published Research
A comprehensive summary of published research on SETD5 Syndrome from 2020 to the present, written for families and caregivers rather than clinicians.

### `setd5-medical-terms-guide.html` — Medical Terms Guide
A plain-language glossary of medical, genetic, and service-related terms that appear in SETD5-related reports, appointments, and research — organized alphabetically.

### `disability-discounts-guide.html` — Disability Discounts Guide
A guide to nationwide discount programs available to disabled children and adults in the United States, plus resources for finding state-specific savings. Primarily US-focused.

---

## Notes for Contributors

- **All pages link back to `index.html`** via a "← Back to Main Page" button in the footer.
- **Medical content** is compiled from published research and is intended for informational purposes only — not as medical advice.
- **US-specific content** (SSI, Medicaid, IEPs, discount programs) is labeled as such on the site. International equivalents are not currently covered.
- **Verify before publishing.** Information about programs, discounts, and research changes over time. Always confirm details with the original source before updating any page.
- The site uses plain HTML and CSS — no frameworks or build tools are required.

---

## Disclaimer

This site is for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for medical advice. Programs and research change — always verify details directly with the provider, agency, or published source.

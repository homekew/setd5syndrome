2a. Claim Verification

Use this to catch overconfident or unqualified claims across medical, scientific, educational, and legal areas. This is the broadest accuracy check and should be run on every page.

Note: This is your first line of defense. It catches claims that may sound reasonable but that a parent without professional credentials should not be making definitively.
Review the following content from a website run by a parent of a child with SETD5 Syndrome. The author is NOT a medical, educational, legal, or any other kind of credentialed professional. This is a peer support and information resource, not a clinical or expert resource. Go through every factual or descriptive claim on the page. For each claim, assign one of these verdicts: VERIFIED — factual, appropriately hedged, suitable for a parent to state NEEDS SOURCE — plausible but requires a citation to be credible NEEDS SOFTENING — stated too definitively; reframe as experience or something families may want to discuss with a professional REMOVE OR REWRITE — crosses into professional advice territory Specifically flag any claim that: MEDICAL & SCIENTIFIC - States or implies a cause, mechanism, prognosis, or treatment outcome as established fact - Recommends, discourages, or evaluates any medical intervention, therapy, medication, or clinical approach - Describes what a diagnosis means for a child's future in definitive terms - Uses clinical language that could be mistaken for medical guidance - Makes statistical or prevalence claims without a source EDUCATIONAL - States what schools will or should do as if it is guaranteed - Describes outcomes of IEPs, therapies, or educational interventions definitively rather than as possibilities or shared experiences - Implies a particular approach is right for all children with SETD5 LEGAL & BENEFITS - Uses 'entitled to', 'have the right to', 'must', or 'required to' regarding schools, insurers, or government programs - Describes eligibility for benefits or services as guaranteed - Gives guidance on appeals or disputes that sounds like legal strategy GENERAL CREDIBILITY - Any claim requiring professional credentials to make responsibly - Any claim a family might act on without consulting a professional, where acting without professional input could cause harm For each flagged item provide: 1.

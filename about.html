About This Site

Why This Site Exists

My son was diagnosed with SETD5 Syndrome at age 11, after years of appointments, testing, and waiting. When the diagnosis finally came, it was a relief to finally have a name for what we'd been navigating, but SETD5 Syndrome is rare enough that there wasn't an easy, ready-made roadmap for us. I found myself searching on my own, reading research papers written in technical language, piecing together information from genetics journals and parent forums.

So I built this site to gather everything I wished I'd had, all in one place, for families like ours. The information is out there, but it's scattered across the internet. I wanted to try and bring it together somewhere accessible.

What You'll Find Here

Guides for newly diagnosed families, summaries of published research written in accessible language, a medical terms glossary, and practical tools like family stories, a handy one-page summary you can download and bring to appointments, resources for navigating IEPs, and more. Everything organized to be useful whether you're a parent, an educator, a medical professional, or someone else in the SETD5 Syndrome community, and shaped with input from families who understand what this journey actually looks like.

One more thing worth saying: I'm not a medical professional or a researcher. Please always check with your child's care team for anything specific to your situation.

Above all, this site is for families like ours. I hope it helps.

Sincerely,

Kate

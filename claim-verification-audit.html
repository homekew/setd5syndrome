Audit standard: This site is written by a non-credentialed author for a patient and family audience. The standard is not whether a statement is clinically accurate, but whether it could be misread as clinical guidance, directional advice, or a verified fact about the current state of medical practice or science.

Section 1
Clinical Recommendation Language

The site cannot make clinical recommendations. Any sentence that tells families what to do medically — or passes through a clinician's recommendation as if it were established fact — must be reframed.

Recommendation verbs
Does any sentence use a recommendation verb? If from a study author, the framing must attribute it to them, not present it as established standard.
recommendedshouldmustis indicatedis requiredis advisedneed toadvise
Action-directing language
Does any callout tell families to request, ask for, pursue, or take a specific clinical action? Families may be told they "can ask" or "may want to discuss" — not that this "provides the basis for requesting."
provides the basis for requestingyou should askrequest a referral
Bare symptom lists
Does any callout list symptoms of a condition without framing them as described in published reports? Bare symptom lists can cause families to self-diagnose.
can cause symptoms including[condition] symptoms are
Correct framing: "Symptoms described in published reports include…" ending with a prompt to discuss with their child's care team.
Section 2
Statistics and Source Attribution

Every specific number, percentage, or prevalence figure on the page must be traceable to a source within that card or section. A reader should never encounter a statistic without knowing where it came from.

Floating statistics
Does the card contain a percentage, fraction, or count that is not attributed to a specific paper within that card? Attribution must appear in the same card, not just the footer or sources page.
approximately X%1 in X patientsX% of SETD5 patients
Multi-gene cohort stats as SETD5-specific
Does the card present statistics from a study of multiple genes as if they apply to SETD5 specifically? If a study included 19 genes and reports "93.9% had developmental delay," that is not a SETD5-specific figure.
X of Y genes identifiedpatients with epigenetic gene variants
Correct framing: "Across all [N] patients representing [N] genes including SETD5…" with a note that percentages reflect the full multi-gene group.
Small cohort statistics
Are statistics from studies of 30 or fewer patients presented without noting the cohort size? Small-cohort percentages are highly unstable and should always include "in a cohort of [N] patients."
28 patients13 participantscase series of [small N]
Section 3
Superlative and Definitive Claims

The site cannot make superlative claims about the state of the literature or definitive claims about what a condition "is." These statements can be invalidated by new research or interpreted as authoritative by readers.

Superlative language
Does any sentence use a superlative? These require either a citation or a hedge such as "as of this page's last review" or "among the most."
most comprehensivemost direct waythe onlythe largestmost importantmost commonlymost widelybest
Definitive clinical characterizations
Does any sentence state a clinical fact as absolute — without hedging — when it reflects current scientific consensus rather than established medical standard? Example: "Autism in SETD5 is a direct feature of the condition" vs. "Research suggests autism in SETD5 may reflect the condition itself."
is a direct featureis not a separate diagnosisis caused bydefinitivelyis a direct result

Does any sentence claim that unnamed "clinicians," "researchers," or "experts" have done or recommended something? These cannot be verified and should be removed or replaced with a direct reference to a named study.
some clinicians have citedresearchers recommendexperts suggest

Current program offerings

enrollment is freeincludes access tooffers [specific service]

Does the page commit to a specific update frequency? Replace with "reviewed and updated regularly" unless there is a verified process to ensure that schedule is met.

Therapeutic Implications

Research findings about biological mechanisms are frequently framed in ways that imply therapeutic relevance. This framing must not suggest that treatments exist, are imminent, or are in active clinical development unless that is verifiably true.

Implied treatments
Does any sentence suggest that treatments exist or are available when they do not? "Treatments that restore SETD5 function may be relevant" implies treatments exist. Correct framing: "If treatments targeting X are developed in the future, they may be relevant."

become a target of ongoing researchbeing studied as a treatmentin active development
Animal model generalization
Does any callout draw a direct line from animal model findings to human treatment without the required caveats? Animal model findings require: (a) noting the model type, and (b) noting that findings have not been validated in human clinical trials.

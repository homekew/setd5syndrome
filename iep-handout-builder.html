What is SETD5 Syndrome? SETD5 Syndrome is a rare genetic condition caused by a loss-of-function variant in the SETD5 gene (chromosome 3p25.3). It typically causes intellectual disability, global developmental delay, and speech and language difficulties. Features vary widely between individuals. The items below reflect this child's profile.

What is SETD5 Syndrome? SETD5 Syndrome is a rare genetic condition caused by a loss-of-function variant in the SETD5 gene (chromosome 3p25.3), resulting in haploinsufficiency. It typically causes intellectual disability, global developmental delay, and speech and language difficulties. Most cases arise de novo (not inherited), though autosomal dominant inheritance is possible. Features vary widely — the items checked below reflect this child's profile.

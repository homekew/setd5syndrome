2026
Review SETD Enzyme Family and Immune Function
Frontiers in Immunology, Jan 2026
Review article examining how SETD family enzymes, including SETD5, function in immune regulation and inflammatory pathways. Provides broader biological context for understanding SETD5's role beyond neurodevelopment.
Overlap 6 Patients Initially Diagnosed With Noonan Syndrome Found to Have SETD5
Kim et al. · Am J Med Genet, 2026
Six patients originally diagnosed with Noonan Syndrome were found to carry SETD5 variants instead, highlighting diagnostic overlap between these conditions and the importance of genetic testing.
Case Report SETD5 Identified as Candidate for Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia
Heidargholizadeh et al. · Pediatric International, Jan 2026
SETD5 variants identified in patients with congenital diaphragmatic hernia through exome sequencing, extending the range of structural anomalies potentially associated with the gene.
2025
Cohort National Brain Gene Registry: 13 Patients — New Brain and Musculoskeletal Features
Callahan et al. · Clinical Genetics, 2025
13 individuals aged 2–37 documented through the National Brain Gene Registry, revealing brain structural differences and musculoskeletal problems at higher rates than previously reported.
Cohort 28 New Patients — Largest Single SETD5 Cohort Study
De Falco et al. · Eur J Paed Neurol, 2024 (pub 2025)
Multicenter European study describing 28 previously unreported SETD5 patients. Found 75% had ID or global developmental delay, 21% had borderline intellectual functioning, and 3.6% had normal IQ — documenting the wide spectrum.
Overlap Molecular Link: SETD5, KBG Syndrome, and Cornelia de Lange Syndrome
bioRxiv preprint, 2025
Identified a shared molecular pathway connecting SETD5, KBG Syndrome (ANKRD11), and Cornelia de Lange Syndrome. May explain overlapping clinical features across these conditions.
Registry Simons Searchlight: Three SETD5 Quarterly Data Reports
Simons Searchlight, 2025
Three quarterly reports published with updated data on enrolled SETD5 families, tracking developmental outcomes, medical features, and family demographics.
2014
Landmark SETD5 First Identified as a Cause of Intellectual Disability
Grozeva et al. · Nature Genetics, 2014
The paper that started it all. Exome sequencing of a large cohort identified SETD5 as a recurrently mutated gene in intellectual disability — the first time SETD5 was linked to a human developmental disorder.
2013
Predecessor 3p25.3 Deletion Mapped — One Year Before SETD5 Identified
2013
A 2.2 Mb deletion at 3p25.3 was mapped in a patient with ID and dysmorphic features, defining the critical region that would later be shown to contain SETD5.

Option A
Tabs live inside the dark hero. Site-nav sits below it, sticky.
The page title and its tabs are grouped together in the dark header (context). The cream site-nav then pins below it as a separate sticky bar. Tabs scroll off the screen — to switch tabs, user scrolls back to top.

Pros

  • Title and tabs feel unified — one cohesive header block

  • Breaks expected behavior (tabs usually stay accessible)
Option B Recommended
Two distinct sticky bars — cream site-nav on top, dark tab-nav below.
The dark hero header scrolls away. Two slim bars pin to the top: cream for site-wide nav, dark for page-specific tabs. They're visually distinct enough that they read as "where am I on the site" vs "what section am I viewing" — not redundant.

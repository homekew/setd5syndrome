This describes what kind of change happened to the DNA. Different types of changes affect the gene in different ways:

Types of genetic changes seen in SETD5

Frameshift

A letter was added or deleted, scrambling all the instructions that follow.

Nonsense

One letter changed in a way that creates a premature stop signal.

Missense

One letter changed, swapping one protein building block for another.

Splice-site

A change at the boundary where the gene's instructions are edited together.

Deletion

A piece of the genetic code is missing. Can be small or large.

Duplication

A section of genetic code is copied, appearing twice instead of once.

Interpreting your results

What the Classification Means

Pathogenic

This change is known to cause disease. Strong evidence from multiple sources. The clearest result a genetic test can give.

Likely Pathogenic

Strong evidence this change causes disease, but seen in fewer people. Most clinicians treat this similarly to Pathogenic.

Variant of Uncertain Significance

Not enough evidence yet. Doesn't confirm or rule out a diagnosis. Can be reclassified as more data becomes available.

Likely Benign / Benign

Evidence suggests this change does not cause disease. Benign variants are common and not associated with your child's condition.

Callouts & Notices
Key Takeaway

SETD5 Syndrome is caused by a pathogenic variant in the SETD5 gene, which plays a critical role in regulating gene expression during early brain development.

International Note

Diagnostic terminology may differ by country. In the UK and Europe, this condition may still appear under older nomenclature in some clinical systems.

Research Note

This summary reflects publications as of early 2026. New studies are added as they are peer-reviewed and indexed in PubMed.

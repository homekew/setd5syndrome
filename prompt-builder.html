Step 1: Who should the AI be?
Role Start here

Give the AI a job title or persona. This shapes the tone and knowledge it draws on, like hiring the right person for the task.

Try: "a patient cooking teacher for beginners" · "a financial advisor who uses plain English" · "a friendly doctor explaining things to a worried parent"

Step 2: What do you want it to do?
Task Most important

Be as specific as you can. The clearer your ask, the better the answer. Include what you want, how much of it, and any key details.

Try: "Write a 5-step guide to..." · "Create a list of 10 ideas for..." · "Explain the difference between X and Y in simple terms"

Step 3: Background info  optional but helpful
Context Optional

What does the AI need to know before it starts? Your situation, what you already have, any constraints. The more relevant detail you give, the better.

Try: "I'm a complete beginner" · "My budget is $500" · "This is for a school project due Friday" · "I've already tried X and it didn't work"

Step 4: Who is this for?  optional
Audience Optional

Who will read or use the output? Knowing the audience helps the AI pitch the language and detail level exactly right.

Try: "busy parents with no medical background" · "a board of executives" · "my 12-year-old daughter" · "someone who has never used a computer"

Step 5: How should it be laid out?  optional
Format Optional

Tell the AI how you want the response structured. Without this, it will choose on its own, which is usually fine.

Try: "a numbered step-by-step guide" · "a comparison table with pros and cons" · "3 short paragraphs" · "slide-by-slide outline with speaker notes"

Step 6: How should it sound?  optional
Tone Optional

Choose how the response should feel. A prompt for a grieving parent needs a different tone than one for a business pitch.

Step 7: How long?  optional
Length Optional

Left blank, the AI will decide on its own, which is often a reasonable choice. Set this if you have a specific length in mind.

Step 8: Any rules to follow?  optional
Rules & Constraints Optional

Anything the AI must include, or must avoid. Think of these as guardrails.

Try: "Don't use bullet points" · "Keep under 200 words" · "Only use publicly available information" · "Do not start with 'Great question!'"

Step 9: Show it what you want  optional but powerful
Example Output Optional

This is one of the most powerful things you can do. Show the AI exactly what a good result looks like. Even one example sentence helps more than you would think.

Try: Describe the structure, write a sentence in the tone you want, or paste a short sample from something you like

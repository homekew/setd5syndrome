About This Guide

The SETD5 Syndrome Companion is a free, peer-built resource created by a parent of a child with SETD5 Syndrome. It was designed to be the resource we wish we'd had at diagnosis.

Everything is written in plain language — no medical jargon, no information overload. Just honest, practical guidance wherever you are in the journey.

The site covers: what SETD5 Syndrome is, commonly reported features, genetics and inheritance, early intervention, IEPs and school services, medical specialists, research registries, and more.

The Basics

What is SETD5 Syndrome?

SETD5 Syndrome is a rare genetic neurodevelopmental condition caused by a change (variant) in the SETD5 gene on chromosome 3. This gene helps regulate how other genes are switched on and off during early development.

When one copy doesn't function correctly, it can lead to a range of developmental, behavioral, medical, and physical features. No two individuals are exactly alike — severity and presentation vary widely.

Quick Facts
Gene SETD5 (chromosome 3p25.3)
First described 2014
Inheritance Usually de novo (new mutation); sometimes inherited
Also known as SETD5 haploinsufficiency

Early intervention — including speech, occupational, and physical therapy — can make a significant difference. Early is always better.

What Families Report

Commonly Reported Features

Not every individual will have all of these. Features vary widely in type and severity.

Learning & Cognition
  • Intellectual disability (varies)
  • Global developmental delay
  • Working memory difficulties
  • Reading & numeracy challenges
Speech & Language
  • Speech & language delay
  • Comprehension often stronger than expressive speech
  • Some minimally verbal
  • Stuttering or articulation differences
Behavior & Regulation
  • ADHD features
  • Anxiety; difficulty with transitions
  • Autism spectrum features
  • Sensory sensitivities
  • Preference for routine
Social & Adaptive
  • Social communication challenges
  • Difficulty with safety awareness
  • Often described as happy & sociable
Neurological & Motor
  • Low muscle tone (hypotonia)
  • Gross & fine motor delays
  • Coordination difficulties
  • Epilepsy or seizures
  • Sleep disturbances
Medical & Physical
  • Feeding difficulties in infancy
  • Growth concerns; short stature
  • Eye or vision concerns
  • Congenital heart defect
  • Bone, joint, or skeletal concerns

Features listed reflect published research as of 2026. For informational purposes only — not medical advice. Always consult your care team.

After Diagnosis

Where to Start

1
See a Genetics Specialist

A geneticist or genetic counselor can explain the specific variant, discuss inheritance, and answer questions about recurrence.

2
Request a Developmental Evaluation

Early evaluation identifies areas where therapy can help most. Ask your pediatrician for a referral.

3
Enroll in Simons Searchlight

Free registry offering genetic counseling and connecting your family to SETD5 research. No cost to participate.

4
Connect with the Community

Other families are your most valuable resource. Find the SETD5 Syndrome Facebook group and the Family Toolkit at our site.

Background #EFEEEA, left border 4px solid #2A627A. Label: Poppins 11px / 600 / uppercase / color #A07D54. Body: 15px / line-height 1.68. Used for notes and supplementary info.
Note: Moyamoya disease

Moyamoya disease has been reported in a very small number of individuals with SETD5 Syndrome. Families may want to discuss this association with their neurologist if unexplained neurological symptoms arise.

Amber callout
.amber-callout
Background #F5EDE0, left border 4px solid #A07D54. Label color: #9E7545. Used for important notices or warnings.
Important note

This list describes features reported in people with SETD5 Syndrome. It is not a prediction of what any individual will experience. Every person with SETD5 Syndrome is different.

▶ Note on Orphanet reclassification (2026)

Orphanet previously had a standalone entry for this condition. On March 1, 2026, Orphanet marked that entry obsolete and merged it into a broader category. This is a database reclassification decision and does not affect the diagnosis.

.steps-list / .step-item / .step-number

Genetics and genetic counseling

Many families find this to be a helpful early step. A genetic counselor can help explain what the variant means for your specific situation.

2

Specialist referrals

Given the range of systems SETD5 Syndrome can affect, care teams sometimes include a neurologist, cardiologist, and audiologist.

Cards

Specialist card
.specialist-grid / .specialist-card
2-column grid (1-col on mobile <560px). White card, border #DDD8D1, border-radius: 8px. Title: Poppins 14.5px / 600 / navy. Body: 14px / muted. Gap between cards: 10px.
Medical Geneticist

Explains the variant, inheritance, and recurrence risk. Often the first specialist after diagnosis.

Developmental Pediatrician

Oversees overall developmental care and coordinates specialists for children.

Neurologist

Evaluates and manages seizures, EEG findings, and neurological features.

Speech-Language Pathologist

Addresses speech, language, communication, and swallowing difficulties.
